A Complete Guide to Buying a New Boiler

If you have central heating in your home, then it’s most likely the biggest contributor to your monthly bills. Boilers are a big household purchase that rightly so, take some consideration.

With that being the case it’s important you buy the right boiler and that your boiler is well maintained. According to the Energy Saving Trust, heating and hot water accounts on average for 62% of a household’s bills. So how do you make the decision on which boiler to buy and who to buy from when your purchase has such a large impact on your monthly costs?
Here at Aquaheat we appreciate how tough it is to be left without heating and hot water so we’ve created an easy to follow 3 step guide to buying a new boiler. We hope the below makes it simpler to buy your new boiler but any questions please send our boiler experts a message via our contact form

Step 1

Can your current boiler be repaired?

 

Getting left without heating or hot water is always less than ideal. Before jumping straight into getting a new boiler have you found Gas Safe boiler engineers that can try repair your current boiler first? At Aquaheat, our Gas Safe boiler engineers can help with all aspects of boiler repairs and maintenance, and we'll always try and fix your boiler problem first, however if we believe you might need a new boiler, we'll offer you the best price on a new boiler installation.

 

Finding a company who specialise in boiler repairs means they can diagnose the issue, offer you an instant quote and, if you’re happy, carry out the repairs quickly and effectively there and then. A fast solution to getting your boiler back up and running effectively.

 

Aquaheat offer a range of boiler services from our expert engineers that could keep your boiler running effectively without the need to buy a new one.
 

  • Boiler servicing to manufacturers standards
  • Strip and clean all appropriate parts
  • Test gas pipework installation in property for leaks
  • Check boiler is using correct volume of gas
  • Check flue emissions with flue analyser
  • Check gas supply pressure
